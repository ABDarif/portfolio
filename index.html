About Me

I’m a developer who loves building accessible, pixel-perfect, user interfaces. I most enjoy building software in the sweet spot where design and engineering meet — things that look good, built well under the hood but most importantly, cool to a novice.


Currently, I'm a Sophomore in the department of Computer Science & Engineering at BRAC University. Also, I am the current Vice-Chair at the IEEE Robotics & Automation Society BRAC University Student Branch Chapter. Here, I help host, manage & organize seminars, workshops & Industry Visits with the great minds & technologically advanced places behind the fast-growing Robotics sector in Bangladesh.


Previously, I had the privilege of being a member at the Laboratory of Space Systems Engineering & Technology (LaSSET), which was solely responsible for launching the first ever nano-satellite of Bangladesh, BRAC Onnesha. I worked at the project named BRACU ALTER there & was a Senior Contributor at the Artificial Intelligence & Firmware Sub-team. Prior to that, I worked at another flagship robotics project of BRAC University, BRACU Dichari, which placed 4th & won the “Perseverance Award” at the European Robotics League 2022 in Poland. I worked as a Junior Contributor at the Control & Software Sub-team.


In my spare time, I’m usually playing video games, reading fiction books, hanging out with my friends & families, or roaming around my town & countryside on my bicycle
